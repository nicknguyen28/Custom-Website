About Me

I am originally from St. Paul, Minnesota, but my family and I moved to Texas before I even turned one. Since then, I have been living in Austin, Texas, up until moving to College Station for school. I am currently a third-year student at Texas A&M University pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science degree. My interest in computers has been a part of my life since I was a kid. I am fascinated by multiple aspects of computers such as the actual computer parts, software, and video games.

I love constantly learning about new things and finding different ways to solve problems, which is another reason why I enjoy computer science and know it is the perfect path for me. In both school and my free time, I realize that I feel most fulfilled with what I am doing when it requires me to really think and find solutions to any issues or puzzles I may come across. My passion for learning and problem solving is fueled by the abundant amount of coding languages and challenging coding exercises available and taught by the university.

Although I have not completed many personal projects, I plan on completing many of them in the near future in addition to learning several more languages. Doing so will allow me to gain experience and pursue my goal as a software programmer. I dream of a future as a software programmer because the idea of using code to create amazing things, solve difficult problems, and more to make a huge positive impact on society excites me. I look forward to using my expertise in computer science to give back to my community on a large scale. I want to help others feel the happiness and excitement I first felt when I experienced the work of software programmers when I was younger, whether they realize the true extent of what led to the final product or not.
